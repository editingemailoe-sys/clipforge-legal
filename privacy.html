ClipForge - Privacy Policy

Last updated: 26 June 2026

ClipForge ("the app") is a private, self-hosted tool used by its individual operator to repurpose their own long-form videos into short vertical clips and upload them to their own connected social media accounts. This policy explains what data the app touches and how it is handled.

Who this app is for

ClipForge is operated privately by a single user on their own computer. It is not a public service and does not have other end users. The only person whose accounts it connects to is its operator.

What data we access

  • TikTok account info (open id, display name, avatar) via the user.info.basic scope - used only to show which account is connected.
  • Video upload permission (video.upload scope) - used only to send videos the operator created into the operator's own TikTok drafts, which the operator then reviews and posts inside the TikTok app.

What we store and where

Access tokens are stored locally on the operator's own machine and are used solely to communicate with the official TikTok API. No account data, tokens, or videos are sent to any third-party server operated by us, because there is no such server - the app runs entirely on the operator's computer.

What we do NOT do

  • We do not sell, share, or transfer any personal data to third parties.
  • We do not use the data for advertising or profiling.
  • We do not access any account other than the operator's own connected account.

Data retention and deletion

Tokens remain on the operator's machine until the operator disconnects the account or deletes the local token file, at which point they are removed. The operator can also revoke ClipForge's access at any time from their TikTok account's connected-apps settings.
